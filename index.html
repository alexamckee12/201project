What are Carbs, Proteins, and Fats?

  • Carbs

    Healthy carbs like whole grains such as quinoa, oats, and brown rice provide a steady release of energy throughout the day. Sweet potatoes and other root vegetables are excellent sources of fiber and essential vitamins. Fruits such as apples, bananas, and berries offer natural sugars, fiber, and various nutrients that support overall health.

  • Proteins

    Proteins from lean meats like chicken breast and turkey offer high-quality protein without excessive saturated fat. Plant-based proteins such as lentils, chickpeas, and tofu provide essential amino acids for vegetarians and vegans. Fish such as salmon and tuna are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and complete proteins for heart and brain health.

  • Fats

    Fats from avocados are packed with healthy monounsaturated fats that promote heart health and provide a creamy texture to meals. Nuts and seeds, such as almonds and chia seeds, offer a good source of essential fats and protein. Olive oil is a versatile, heart-healthy fat that can be used for cooking and dressing salads.

Hawaiian Pizza

47g C/13g F/43g P

  • Ingredients: - 1 3/4 cup self-rising flour - 1 cup nonfat plain Greek yogurt - ½ cup Primal Kitchen Hawaiian BBQ Sauce - 1 ½ cups part-skim shredded mozzarella - 1 pound MFF Huli Huli Grilled Chicken (get the recipe at macrofriendlyfood.app), diced - 8 ounces fresh pineapple, cut into bite-sized pieces - 2 ounces thinly sliced red onion - 4 slices Hormel Original Precooked Bacon, reheated and crumbled
  • Instructions: 1. Preheat oven to 400°F. 2. Cover a large baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside. 3. In a mixing bowl or stand mixer, combine flour and yogurt until a thick dough forms. 4. If dough is too dry, add 1-2 tablespoons of water and mix until the dough pulls away from the edge of the bowl. 5. Roll dough out onto a lightly floured surface into a rectangle (approximately 10"x14") then place on the prepared baking sheet and set aside. 6. Spread BBQ sauce over crust in an even layer and top with mozzarella, chicken, pineapple, red onion, and bacon. 7. Bake for 20-22 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese has melted. 8. Cut into slices and serve.

Shrimp Tacos

10g C/2g F/13g P

  • Ingredients: - 1 lb. large or jumbo shrimp, peeled (fresh or frozen and thawed) - 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided - 1 tsp chili powder - 1 tsp ground chipotle chili pepper - 1 tsp ground cumin - 1/2 tsp kosher salt - 6 to 8 corn or flour tortillas - Garnishes: avocados, cilantro, lime wedges, salsa - 1 cup nonfat plain Greek yogurt - 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil - 1 clove garlic, peeled - 1/2 small jalapeño, seeds and membranes removed - 1/4 cup tightly packed fresh cilantro leaves - 1/4 tsp kosher salt - Zest and juice of 1 large lime (2 tsp zest and 3 tbsp juice) - 2 cups shredded cabbage or slaw
  • Instructions: 1. Toss the shrimp with 1/2 tbsp of olive oil, chili powder, ground chipotle chili pepper, cumin, and salt. 2. Heat the remaining 1/2 tbsp of olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned shrimp and cook until pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side. 3. For the sauce, blend Greek yogurt, olive oil, garlic, jalapeño, cilantro, salt, lime zest, and juice until smooth. 4. Toss the shredded cabbage or slaw with a portion of the sauce. 5. Assemble tacos by placing the shrimp on tortillas, then topping with slaw and additional sauce. 6. Garnish with avocados, cilantro, lime wedges, and salsa, if desired.

Salmon Bowls

22g C/12g F/28g P

  • Ingredients: 5 salmon fillets (4-6 oz each) 2 cups dry jasmine rice (or use white rice, sushi rice, brown rice, or coconut rice) 1 1/2 cups edamame, shelled 1 English cucumber, sliced 6 green onions, chopped 2 mangos, peeled and cubed 2 avocados, peeled, seeded, and cubed 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro Sriracha mayo* for topping (optional) Teriyaki Sauce ½ cup low-sodium soy sauce 2 tablespoons rice vinegar 1 tablespoon sesame oil 1/4 cup + 1 tablespoon light brown sugar 1 tablespoon honey ¾ teaspoon ground ginger 1 clove garlic, minced 2 teaspoons cornstarch + 2 teaspoons water, mixed together to make a cornstarch slurry 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • Instructions: 1. Make Teriyaki Sauce: Add all of the sauce ingredients to a small saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, and boil for about 1 minute. The sauce should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Allow to cool. Marinate Salmon: Pour ¼ cup of the teriyaki sauce over the raw salmon and set aside to marinate for at least 20 minutes or up to overnight. Cook rice according to package instructions. 2. Cook Salmon: (I prefer the air fryer, but see baking and grilling instructions in notes.) Preheat air fryer to 400°F. Spray basket with non-stick spray and place salmon fillets inside. Air fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until cooked through (flakes easily with a fork, or about 125-135 degrees if testing with a thermometer). Assemble Salmon Bowls: Divide rice among the bowls and top with a piece of teriyaki salmon, some avocado, edamame, mango, and cucumber. 3. Garnish: Drizzle teriyaki sauce and Sriracha mayo over the top and garnish with chopped green onion and cilantro.

Southwest Salad

12g C/4g F/24g P

  • Ingredients: 5 salmon fillets (4-6 oz each) 2 cups dry jasmine rice (or use white rice, sushi rice, brown rice, or coconut rice) 1 1/2 cups edamame, shelled 1 English cucumber, sliced 6 green onions, chopped 2 mangos, peeled and cubed 2 avocados, peeled, seeded, and cubed 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro Sriracha mayo* for topping (optional) Teriyaki Sauce ½ cup low-sodium soy sauce 2 tablespoons rice vinegar 1 tablespoon sesame oil 1/4 cup + 1 tablespoon light brown sugar 1 tablespoon honey ¾ teaspoon ground ginger 1 clove garlic, minced 2 teaspoons cornstarch + 2 teaspoons water, mixed together to make a cornstarch slurry 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • Instructions: For the Pico de Gallo: combine ingredients in a small bowl then set aside. Can be done a day ahead of time. Stir together desired amounts of Ranch dressing and salsa (I used roughly 50/50 ratios) in a small bowl to create Salsa-Ranch dressing, then set aside. Add romaine, sweet corn, black beans, and desired amount of pico de gallo to a large bowl then toss to combine. Add avocado, crushed tortilla chips, and Salsa-Ranch dressing then toss again to combine. Serve immediately.

Chicken Pesto Panini

22g C/22g F/18g P

  • Ingredients: 1 focaccia bread, quartered ½ cup prepared basil pesto 1 cup diced cooked chicken ½ cup diced green bell pepper ¼ cup diced red onion 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • Instructions: 1. Preheat a panini grill. 2. Slice each quarter of focaccia bread in half horizontally. Spread each half with pesto. Layer bottom halves with equal amounts chicken, bell pepper, onion, and cheese. Top with remaining focaccia halves, forming 4 sandwiches. 3. Grill paninis 5 minutes in the preheated grill, or until focaccia bread is golden brown and cheese is melted.

Chicken Wrap

35g C/20g F/24g P

  • Ingredients: Sour cream Salsa Taco seasoning For the Wraps: Tortillas – Make sure to use large tortillas for these wraps. Lettuce – You can use any kind of lettuce, but I like to use iceberg for extra crunch. Tomatoes Avocado Cheddar cheese – For best results, shred your own cheese. However, pre-shredded cheese will also work. Cooked chicken Fritos
  • Instructions: * Make the sauce. Put the salsa, sour cream, and taco seasoning in a bowl, and mix them together. * Sauce the tortilla. Lay a tortilla on your cutting board and spread sauce over the bottom half. Leave a dry border around the edge of the tortilla. * Add the ingredients. Place the lettuce, tomatoes, avocado, cheese, and chicken on top of the sauce. Then crush Fritos over the top of the wrap, and drizzle more sauce on top. * Fold. Fold in the sides of the tortilla and roll it tightly. Repeat with the rest of the tortillas, and cut in half before serving.
